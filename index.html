Bangladesh's Global Winners & Achievements

Bangladesh has produced 4 global champions and 1 finalist honorable mention — proof that our teams can compete and win on the world stage.

2018 — SUST, Sylhet, Bangladesh 🇧🇩

Team Olik — Best Use of Data (Global Champion)

Students from Shahjalal University of Science and Technology won the global "Best Use of Data" award for "Virtual Space Exploration," a lunar VR moon-landing simulator — marking Bangladesh's historic first-ever global championship.

2020 — BUET, Dhaka, Bangladesh 🇧🇩

Team BUET Zenith — Global Finalist (Honorable Mention)

"Satellight" (Orbital Sky challenge) — a first-person Earth view streamed from satellites, a VR satellite-finder app, and an Arduino device that physically points to a satellite's live position in the sky.

2021 — KUET & BAUET, Khulna, Bangladesh 🇧🇩

Team Mohakash — Best Mission Concept (Global Champion)

Won the global "Best Mission Concept" award for an interactive 3D lunar regolith-collection scoop — the start of Bangladesh's three-year championship streak.

2022 — Cumilla, Bangladesh 🇧🇩

Team Diamonds — Most Inspirational

"Diamond in the Sky" — won the global "Most Inspirational" award. An inspiring celestial pattern-matching game that won the hearts of global judges.

2023 — Rajshahi, Bangladesh 🇧🇩

TeamVoyagers — Best Storytelling (Global Champion)

"Aqua Explorer" — won the global "Best Storytelling" award with an interactive game teaching the global hydrological cycle, completing Bangladesh's historic hat-trick of consecutive world championships.
